  • "E-waste?!"

    E-waste is the fastest-growing waste stream on the planet E-waste refers to discarded electrical or electronic devices. These devices contain a mix of valuable materials like gold, copper, and aluminium, alongside hazardous substances like lead, mercury, and flame retardants.

  • "Proper E-waste Management Importance"

    Improper e-waste disposal can have serious environmental consequences.Leaking toxins from landfills can contaminate soil and water, while burning e-waste releases harmful pollutants into the environment. Responsible e-waste management helps to:
    • Conserve natural resources by recovering valuable materials for reuse.
    • Reduce pollution by minimizing the need for raw material extraction and preventing toxic releases.
    • Protect human health by ensuring safe handling and disposal of hazardous materials.

  • "Facts and Figures about E-waste"


    • The World Health Organization (WHO) states that e-waste is the fastest-growing waste stream in the world.
    • According to a United Nations report, in 2019 only a fraction (less than 25%) of e-waste was formally recycled globally.
    • The Basel Convention is an international treaty designed to control the transboundary movements of hazardous wastes, including e-waste.

  • "E-waste Management Regulations and Initiatives"

    Many countries and regions have legislation in place to govern e-waste management. These regulations may include:
    • Bans on the landfilling or incineration of certain types of e-waste.
    • Take-back programs offered by manufacturers or retailers.
    • Extended producer responsibility (EPR) schemes, which hold manufacturers responsible for the collection and recycling of their products.

  • "Practical Tips for Recycling E-waste"


    • Know before you throw: Not all electronics are considered e-waste.exclamation Research what items require special disposal in your area.
    • Find a certified e-waste collector: Look for reputable recyclers who are certified to handle hazardous materials.
    • Wipe your data: Before recycling electronic devices, ensure you have properly erased all personal information.
    • Remove batteries: Batteries often contain hazardous materials and should be recycled separately.
